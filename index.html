David Brown

Senior Director, ASIC Physical Design Methodology and Tools Team

David Brown is Senior Director of the ASCII (Advanced Silicon and Chip Implementation) team at NVIDIA, where he leads efforts to deliver chip designs with the highest performance, lowest power consumption, and optimal yield. His team’s scope spans floorplanning to final design closure, with deep focus on gate-level power optimization, timing closure, constraint management, AI-driven automation, and engineering productivity.

David brings rare breadth across both hardware and software: his background covers CAD algorithm design (static timing, placement, routing, formal methods), high-performance software architecture, parallel computing, and large-scale data-oriented systems. He has applied classical and machine learning techniques including reinforcement learning, evolutionary algorithms, and deep learning to chip design optimization, and has hands-on design experience across dozens of tapeouts.

In this talk, David will share what his team works on, what kinds of students and engineers they are actively recruiting, and how to think about high-impact careers at the intersection of AI, software, and chip design. An open Q&A will follow.
